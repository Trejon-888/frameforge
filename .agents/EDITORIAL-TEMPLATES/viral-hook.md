# Editorial Template: Viral Hook

**Intent:** Hold attention from frame 1. The viewer who would have swiped at second 2 is still watching at second 15. The viewer at second 15 is curious enough to reach second 45. The viewer at second 45 follows.

**Overlay Count:** 8–14
**Pacing:** Fast. Rhythmic. Relentless in the hook window. Breathing room is earned, not given.
**Visual Density:** High in act 1 (hook window), medium through proof, deliberate in CTA.
**Platform Fit:** TikTok (primary), Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, Twitter/X video

---

## Why This Template Works

Viral short-form video earns attention in three phases: hook (0-3s), retention (3-20s), and reward (20-end). Each phase has a different job.

**Hook:** Interrupt the scroll. Create a question in the viewer's mind that only the next 30 seconds can answer. The visual system creates urgency, energy, surprise.

**Retention:** Deliver on the hook's promise. Keep the visual interest high enough that leaving feels like a cost. Every 5-8 seconds, something new.

**Reward:** Give the viewer something worth sharing or acting on. The CTA should feel like a natural culmination — "I've been on a journey with this creator for 45 seconds, of course I'll follow."

The pattern interrupt is the single highest-leverage edit decision in this template. The first visual event must be unexpected — a color flash, a giant word, an unexpected stat. Once the pattern is broken, the viewer is yours.

---

## Edit Architecture

### Act 1: The Hook Window (0 – 15% of video)

This window is where the battle is won or lost. Pack it. 3-4 overlays in the first 10-15% of a short-form video is appropriate and expected.

**Overlay 1: Pattern Interrupt (0–1.5s)**
*Technique: split-flash OR word-slam (instant)*
The very first visual event. Either a full-frame color flash (0.35s) that snaps into focus, or a single giant word that appears at t=300ms. This should feel like a TV commercial edit cut — sudden, confident, immediately interesting.
- Position: full-frame (center or full-canvas)
- Duration: 800–2000ms (short — it's a punctuation mark, not a statement)
- The word or flash color must relate directly to the video thesis

**Overlay 2: Opening Claim Visual (3–8s)**
*Technique: kinetic-phrase OR large-text slam*
After the pattern interrupt, land the opening claim. The speaker is saying something surprising or bold — make it visible. Sequential word entry works well here: WORD → WORD → WORD arriving in quick succession.
- Position: headline zone or full-center
- Duration: 4000–5500ms

**Overlay 3: First Credibility Signal (8–15s)**
*Technique: social-proof-band OR number-reveal (small)*
Before the viewer asks "who is this?", answer it. Not a formal lower-third (that's authority-builder territory) — a punchy, fast, credibility statement. "$47K months." "300 clients automated." "3 years, 0 employees."
- Position: lower-center or stat zone
- Duration: 3500–4500ms

**Overlay 4: Tension Builder (optional, 12–18s)**
*Technique: ambient-watermark OR second word-slam*
If the hook window needs more energy before releasing into the proof section, add a visual that builds anticipation. A repeated word in the background, a "BUT FIRST..." statement, a countdown element.

### Act 2: Sustained Visual Interest (15% – 75% of video)

The viewer is staying. Now deliver. One overlay every 6-8 seconds on average. Vary the technique — don't use the same type twice in a row.

**Overlays 5-9: Proof + Proof + Proof**
Each overlay corresponds to a concrete claim. In a viral hook template, every proof point gets a visual — there's no empty frames during the "here's why this works" section.

Technique rotation (vary these to avoid visual monotony):
- number-reveal → social-proof-band → word-slam → ui-panel → quote-pull
- Never the same technique twice in a row

**Overlay N: The Peak (at the highest energy moment)**
*Technique: hero number-reveal OR split-flash + stat*
The moment in the proof section with the highest emphasis weight (check transcript intelligence `emphasis[]`). This is the moment that will end up in a screenshot shared on Twitter. Design it for shareability — clear, large, unmistakable.

### Act 3: Compression + CTA (75% – 100% of video)

**Overlay N-1: Outro Energy Shift**
*Technique: canvas-animation OR ambient*
The video is wrapping up. Give it a visual energy shift — orbits, particles, waveform — to signal "we're coming to something." 10-12 seconds.

**Overlay N: CTA (urgent, personal)**
*Technique: cta-card*
The CTA in this template is more urgent than authority-builder. Not just "follow" but "follow IF..." or "link in bio for..." — give the viewer a reason that's specific to this video's content.
- Duration: 7000–10000ms
- Text: verb-first, specific, value-focused

## Timing Formula

For a 60-second video:
```
0.3s    → Pattern interrupt (split-flash or word-slam)
4s      → Opening claim visual
9s      → First credibility signal
16s     → First proof overlay
22s     → Second proof overlay (different technique)
29s     → Third proof overlay
35s     → [breathing room — deliberate pause before peak]
38s     → Peak stat / hero moment
44s     → Quick proof confirmation
48s     → Outro energy shift (canvas)
52s     → CTA card (urgent, specific)
```

For a 30-second video: 5-7 overlays, pack the first 10 seconds, one proof, immediate CTA.

---

## The Hook Test

Before finalizing the first 8 seconds of overlays, ask:
1. Would someone who doesn't know this creator feel a reason to keep watching after second 3?
2. Does the pattern interrupt create a question in the viewer's mind?
3. Is there a visual within the first 5 seconds that would stop a thumb mid-scroll?

If any answer is no, the hook window needs work. The rest of the template is irrelevant if the hook fails.

---

## Quality Signals

**This template is working when:**
- The first 10 seconds feel like 3 seconds
- The viewer learns something unexpected before the 15-second mark
- The visual energy never drops to zero for more than 6 seconds in the proof section
- The CTA feels like an obvious next step, not an interruption

**This template is failing when:**
- The first visual event is a lower-third identity (this is authority-builder, not viral hook)
- There's more than 7 seconds of empty canvas before the first overlay
- The same overlay technique is used 3+ times
- The CTA appears without any outro build-up
